FAQs

Q: What kind of lotus root is 'Shirohana Iwakuni Lotus Root'?

A:Shirohana lotus root is a traditional Iwakuni lotus root, which has been cultivated in the region for a long time. As advancements in breeding have led to the creation of various lotus root varieties, the number of farmers growing Shirohana lotus root has decreased over time. Shirohana lotus root is soft and flavorful, but it is also susceptible to diseases, has low yields, and is difficult to cultivate. Therefore, it is a type of lotus root that can be enjoyed specifically because of Yumiya's efforts.

Q:What is the nutritional value of Iwakuni lotus root?

A:Iwakuni lotus root is low in calories, yet rich in dietary fiber, vitamins, and minerals. It is particularly high in dietary fiber, vitamin C, and potassium.

Q:What is the storage method for Iwakuni lotus root?

A:Iwakuni lotus root is best stored in a cool, dark place. It should be kept in the vegetable compartment of the refrigerator or in a cool area, avoiding moisture and sunlight.

Q:How do you cook Iwakuni lotus root?

A:Iwakuni lotus root can be enjoyed in various cooking methods. You can eat it raw in salads or seasoned dishes, or add it to simmered dishes or stir-fries for a delicious meal.

Q:What is the cultivation period of Iwakuni lotus root?

A:Iwakuni lotus root is cultivated during the growing season from spring to fall. The growth period lasts approximately 4 to 6 months, and the harvest season is from summer to fall.

Q:Does Iwakuni lotus root have any health benefits?

A:Iwakuni lotus root has health benefits such as promoting digestion, relieving constipation, and stabilizing blood sugar levels. Additionally, it is rich in nutrients such as dietary fiber, vitamin C, and potassium, which contribute to maintaining health and have beauty benefits as well.
